About Me


Stephanie is a scientist and researcher focusing on water issues and their intersection with human prosperity. This path emerged when her love of languages and conversation exposed her to the range of challenges that different communities face. She believes that addressing a problem starts by identifying a pattern and giving it a name that translates for everyone.

Stephanie graduated from Carleton College with a major in Middle East history that had also stressed the consequences of water scarcity. After working in a law firm for two years, she returned to school and obtained a masters of environmental and water resources engineering from Tufts University. Following graduation, she worked with the Boston office of the Stockholm Environment Institute, where her projects included data analysis for drinking water quality in Bangalore, India, modeling the Vjosa River in Albania, and supporting stakeholders worldwide in using the water balance system Water Evaluation and Planning (WEAP).

As a researcher, Stephanie has published book chapters and academic articles, including her 2015 paper using water insecurity as a statistical determinant of price elasticity for domestic water demand. The International Water Resources Association awarded it Honorable Mention for Best Paper for its relevance, rigor and impact.

Stephanie lives in Somerville MA and enjoys baking, writing, running and boxing.
